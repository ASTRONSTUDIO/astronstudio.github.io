Q:

What is WaveTurbo?

A:

If your watch is a 2-Band radio that only supports Japanese radio signals, the time after sync the radio signal is Japanese local time, there is a time difference, and it cannot be used in other regions.

Using WaveTurbo, the 2-Band radio watches can sync the local accurate time in any area of the world.

Q:

How to use WaveTurbo?

A:

1. Refer to the manual to set the watch city to the city in Japan. (For example, Tokyo TYO, most of the 2-Band of the radio watches default to the Japanese city without setting)


2. As with other signal waves, click "Start" to use the speaker or headphones to send waves.


3. Refer to the watch manual and adjust the watch to “Perform manual receive” mode, quickly move the watch close to the speaker or headphones, and wait for synced.

Q:

Can the 2, 3, 4, 5, 6-Band be used?

A:

Yes, it can.

As long as the radio watches can sync the Japanese radio signal, it can be used.

Q:

Why is the sync unsuccessful?

A:

Check if the watch's city setting is a local city in Japan.

Turn up the volume and check if the headphones or speakers are working properly.

Try multiple syncs.

If you need feedback help, please submit in the "settings".

Q:

Why is the watch time out of date after a few days?

A:

Receiving the radio signal from Japan, the watch synced the local time in Japan.

You can use WaveTurbo again to synchronize.
